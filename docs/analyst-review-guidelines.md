# Analyst Review Guidelines — Geo Market Watch v6.3

## Purpose

This document provides guidance for analysts reviewing trade ideas generated by the Geo Market Watch Exposure Engine.

## Review Criteria

### 1. Evidence Threshold

**High Conviction Requirements:**
- Multiple credible sources confirm event
- Clear causal link to company/sector
- Quantifiable impact potential
- Near-term catalyst

**Medium Conviction Requirements:**
- Credible sources report event
- Plausible causal link
- Impact direction clear, magnitude uncertain

**Low Conviction Requirements:**
- Single source or emerging story
- Causal link speculative
- High uncertainty

### 2. Thesis Clarity

A good trade idea thesis should:
- State the event clearly
- Explain the transmission mechanism
- Identify the specific exposure
- Define the expected outcome

**Example Clear Thesis:**
> "Strait of Hormuz shipping disruption increases tanker rates by reducing effective vessel supply. Frontline (FRO) has high exposure via VLCC fleet."

**Example Unclear Thesis:**
> "Middle East tension might affect shipping."

### 3. Invalidation Logic

Every trade idea must have clear invalidation conditions:

**Strong Invalidation:**
- Specific, observable condition
- Binary test (yes/no)
- Time-bound where possible

**Weak Invalidation:**
- Vague or subjective
- Hard to observe
- No time frame

**Examples:**

| Strong | Weak |
|--------|------|
| "Brent falls below $70" | "Oil prices stabilize" |
| "Shipping traffic normalizes by March 31" | "Tensions ease" |
| "No attack within 30 days" | "Threat fades" |

### 4. Conviction Calibration

**High Conviction:**
- Use for: High probability + high impact
- Position sizing: Full intended allocation
- Review frequency: Weekly

**Medium Conviction:**
- Use for: Moderate probability OR moderate impact
- Position sizing: Half allocation
- Review frequency: Bi-weekly

**Low Conviction:**
- Use for: Watchlist tracking, early signals
- Position sizing: Test size or none
- Review frequency: Monthly

## Review Decision Guide

### Approve

Use when:
- Thesis is clear and logical
- Evidence meets threshold
- Invalidation is specific
- Conviction is justified

### Reject

Use when:
- Thesis is unclear or flawed
- Evidence is insufficient
- Invalidation is missing or weak
- Conviction is unjustified

**Required:** Provide notes explaining why.

### Monitor

Use when:
- Thesis is plausible but unconfirmed
- Need more evidence
- Waiting for catalyst
- Conviction uncertain

### Needs Revision

Use when:
- Thesis has merit but needs work
- Invalidation needs clarification
- Conviction level seems off
- Missing key information

**Required:** Provide notes on what needs fixing.

## Review Notes Best Practices

### For Rejections

Include:
- Specific flaw in thesis
- Missing evidence
- Better invalidation suggestion
- Learning for system improvement

**Example:**
> "Reject: Thesis assumes immediate rate spike, but vessel supply is actually loose. Suggest: Wait for confirmed fixture disruption before entry."

### For Needs Revision

Include:
- What needs clarification
- Suggested improvements
- Questions to answer
- Evidence to gather

**Example:**
> "Needs revision: Invalidation condition too vague. Suggest: 'Brent backwardation disappears' or 'Hormuz traffic returns to 95% of normal'."

## Common Review Patterns

### Maritime Disruption Ideas

**Check:**
- Vessel supply/demand balance
- Alternative route availability
- Insurance market reaction
- Fixture data confirmation

### Energy Infrastructure Ideas

**Check:**
- Actual vs threatened disruption
- Spare capacity availability
- Strategic reserve releases
- Demand elasticity

### Fertilizer Supply Ideas

**Check:**
- Inventory levels
- Plant operating rates
- Import dependency metrics
- Substitution possibilities

## Dashboard Prioritization

Approved ideas are sorted by:

1. **Conviction** — high first
2. **Recency** — newest first
3. **Status** — active first

This ensures analysts see the highest-quality, most relevant ideas first.

## Feedback Loop

Review patterns inform system improvements:

- Frequent rejections → adjust generation rules
- Common revision requests → improve templates
- Conviction mismatches → calibrate scoring

Analyst notes are the primary input for system evolution.
