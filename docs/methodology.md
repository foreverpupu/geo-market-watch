# Methodology

## Overview

Geo Market Watch transforms geopolitical news into structured market research through a disciplined, multi-layer workflow.

## Core Principles

### 1. Fact-Interpretation Separation
- **Confirmed Facts** (Tier 1 sources only)
- **Market Interpretation** (Tier 2 structural analysis)
- **Scenario Analysis** (hypothetical but bounded)

### 2. Source Authentication
Every source is classified before use:
- **Tier 1**: Official statements, wire services, filings, exchange notices
- **Tier 2**: Domain experts, analysts, structural explainers
- **Low-trust**: Extreme rhetoric, unverified claims, viral tone

### 3. Anti-Hype Filtering
- Strong tone alone doesn't disqualify if source provides verifiable variables
- Downrank sources that confuse commentary with reporting
- Re-anchor on independently verified facts when user links are thin

### 4. Market Exposure Translation
Map political/military developments into quantifiable market variables:
- Export volumes and supply shares
- Terminal capacity and vessel classes
- Pipeline dependencies and backup routes
- Revenue exposure by region/sector

## Workflow Steps

1. **Input Validation** - Require news links; flag confidence reduction if missing
2. **Source Classification** - Tier 1/2/low-trust sorting
3. **Event Identification** - Core event, date, geography, choke points
4. **Freshness Verification** - Web check for time-sensitive facts
5. **Macro Assessment** - Rate direction, inflation backdrop, growth sensitivity
6. **Scenario Building** - A/B/C framework with trigger conditions
7. **Transmission Mapping** - Event → commodities/logistics/policy → sectors → watchlist
8. **Watchlist Construction** - Aggressive / Balanced / Defensive buckets
9. **Signal Design** - Concrete trigger signals and invalidation conditions
10. **Structured Output** - 9-section format per output-template.md

## Scenario Framework

### Scenario A: Risk Premium
- Geopolitical shock, no confirmed infrastructure damage
- Market prices risk, not supply disruption

### Scenario B: Supply Shock
- Confirmed damage to key commodity/logistics infrastructure
- Market shifts to direct supply-shock pricing

### Scenario C: Systemic Repricing
- Prolonged disruption, route closure, regional escalation
- Market shifts to inflation and risk-off repricing

## Quality Controls

- **Fog of War Rule**: Contested facts remain contested (`仍存在争议`)
- **Data Fallback**: Use public proxies when key data is paywalled
- **Asymmetry**: Allocate depth to markets with cleanest transmission path
- **No Mechanical Filling**: Quality overrides count; note `(未强行补足)` when needed

## Output Discipline

All outputs follow the 9-section structure:
1. Event snapshot
2. Confirmed facts
3. Market interpretation
4. Scenario analysis
5. Key indicators
6. Three-market watchlist
7. Bucket summaries
8. Short commentary
9. Risk warning
